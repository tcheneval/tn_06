ABOUT

Think North is the portfolio of Tim Cheneval, a UX/Graphic Designer and outdoorsmen, with a slight obsession with hot drinks, great historical literature and the great outdoors.

I currently work as a User Experience Designer for Backcountry.com, an outdoor gear e-retail company. My work includes crafting and maintaining delightful, frictionless, and engaging experiences for our customers.

In my spare time, I typically throw on a pair of climbing shoes, running shoes or my ski gear and head for the mountains. During the summer I can be found backpacking the trails, living out of a tent, or enjoying the atmosphere of local coffee shops.

Agile and Dual Track Delivery, a Beautiful Mess

After a year and a half of working in an agile environment and drinking the scrum koolaid, I think I have finally come to terms with dual track delivery and the issues behind continuous discovery.

After a full day of long workshops with the premier lean business model consultant Marty Cagan, I've gotten a chance to really investigate some of the issues I find common within my team regarding agile and continuous discovery (or the lack thereof). Sitting in a room, firing questions and concerns at the lean guru, I was thinking I would come out with answers.

Thats not how it played out though. I walked out with a new understanding of lean team dynamics, but the clean diagrams and illustrations of the dual track scrum model seemed less and less realistic to me.

This is the start of an article that will span many hundreds of lines. my thoughts section will have pull quotes and images as well. I have no idea how to handle past thoughts but will figure that out eventually.. Until then, the current, or most recent piece will show up here and serve as the thoughts section.
